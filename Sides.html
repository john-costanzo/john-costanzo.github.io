Authentic Thai Peanut Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 4 teaspoons corn oil,
  • 1/2 cup minced red onion,
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic,
  • 1/2 teaspoon hot chili pepper
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar,
  • 1/3 cup peanut butter,
  • 1 cup of water

Method:

  1. In a blender, combine corn oil, onion, garlic and hot chili pepper; blend for 1 minute
  2. Empty the mixture into a saucepan and place over low heat until the mixture starts releasing aroma. Add peanut butter and mix well over low heat.
  3. Add water and let it simmer for 10 minutes or until thickened.
  4. Stir in vinegar and sugar; continue to simmer for another 1-2 minutes. Taste and add more vinegar and sugar if the mixture does not have the sour and sweet taste.
  5. Place sauce in a shallow plate for satay dipping or pour over fresh salad.

    Crispy Cauliflower Buffalo Wings

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 head cauliflower, chopped into bite size pieces
  • 1 cup garbanzo bean (or wheat) flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp. garlic powder (or minced fresh garlic)
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1 tsp. melted butter or ghee
  • 2/3 cup hot sauce

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 450F.
  2. In a medium size bowl, combine flour, water, garlic powder and salt. Whisk together until smooth.
  3. Toss cauliflower into batter making sure to coat each piece completely, then place battered cauliflower on a lightly greased, non-stick baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 15 minutes, tossing half way through.
  5. In the meantime, combine melted butter and hot sauce in a large bowl, stirring.
  6. When cauliflower is done, remove it from the oven and gently toss it in the hot sauce mixture.
  7. Place cauliflower back on the baking sheet and cook for an additional 25 minutes or until it becomes crispy. Allow cauliflower to cool for 15 minutes before serving.
  8. Serve with your favorite creamy dressing.

Yield: Serves 4

Caramelized Onion and Sun-Dried Tomato Dip

Makes 1 cup; serves 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 4 cups sliced mild onions
  • ¼ teaspoon dried rosemary, crumbled
  • 1/8 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons white wine
  • 6 oil-marinated sun-dried tomato halves
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • ½ teaspoon capers (optional!)
  • 6 small pitted black olives (For best results use a good flavorful olive such as a kalamata.)

Method:

  1. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, heat the oil and the butter.
  2. Add the onion; cook, stirring until softened about 2 minutes. Stir in the
  3. the rosemary and thyme. Lower the heat and cook slowly, uncovered, until onions are golden, stirring occasionally, about 45 minutes. [Don't try to hurry the onions; they'll know this and won't caramelize! -jc]
  4. Add the water, wine, sun-dried tomatoes, parsley and capers (if you have to!). Bring to a boil. Remove from heat and transfer to a food processor container fitted with a steel blade. Add the olives. Cover and process until everything is finely chopped.
  5. Variation: Substitute water for the wine.

Champ

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. potatoes, unpeeled
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 4-5 scallions, finely chopped
  • ¼-½ cup butter
  • salt and pepper

Method:

  1. Champ, also known as cally, poundies and pandy, is one of the most famous ways of serving Ireland's best loved vegetable–the potato. Champ is the country's way of serving mashed potatoes.
  2. Heat a pan of salted water and boil the potatoes in their skins until tender. Drain and dry over low heat covered with a paper towel. Peel and mash well.
  3. While the potatoes are cooking, put the milk and chopped scallions into a saucepan, bring to a boil, and simmer for a few minutes. Gradually beat the milk and onion mixture into the mashed potatoes to form a soft, but not sloppy, mixture. Beat in half the butter and searson with salt pepper. Divide among 4 warm plates or bowls. Make a well in the center of each serving, cut the remaining butter into 4 pieces, and put one piece in each of the hollows. Serve immediately.
  4. Cook's notes:
  5. Traditionally, champ would have been served as a main meal, with a glass of milk or buttermilk. Today, however, it is used to acocompany meats such as boiled ham and broiled sausages. Parsley, young nettle tops, peas and fava beans can be substituted for the scallions.
  6. Serves 4.
  7. Prep time: 5 minutes
  8. Cooking time: 20-25 minutes

CHEESE GRITS

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 c. white grits
  • 3/4 tsp. salt
  • 2 1/2 c. boiling water
  • 1/4 lb. cheddar cheese, grated
  • 1 egg, well beaten
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper
  • Paprika

Method:

  1. Gradually stir grits and salt into the boiling water. Cover and cook for about 10 minutes, until grits are done, stirring often.
  2. Add all but 1/3 cup of the grated cheese and the egg. Season with cayenne and stir until mixture is smooth. Cover and cook a little longer until cheese is melted. Pour into a well greased casserole and top with remaining cheese and dust with paprika. Bake in a 325 degree oven for 25 minutes.
  3. Serves 2.

Gougeres

Makes 16

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon white pepper (black works too)
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup all purpose flour
  • 4 eggs at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup to 1 1/2 cups finely shredded gruyere cheese (about 2 oz) - you can experiment with different kinds of cheese

Method:

  1. Place water, butter, salt and pepper in heavy medium saucepan. Heat over medium heat stirring until butter is melted and mixture boils.
  2. Stir in flour all at once. Beat with wooden spoon over heat until begins to form a ball, about 1 minute.
  3. Remove from heat and beat until slightly cooled about 30 seconds.
  4. Add eggs to flour mixture one at a time while beating with electric hand mixer between each addition until mixture has a dull satiny sheen.
  5. Stir in cheese until well blended.
  6. Butter and flour large baking sheet, or line with parchment paper. Drop warm paste by heaping tablespoonfuls onto baking sheet at least 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake until very well browned at 350 degrees, about 35 minutes. It could take longer for them to be well browned.
  8. Remove from oven and transfer to wire rack. Cut tiny slit in side of each puff to allow steam to escape.
  9. These are best eaten the day they are baked. You can freeze them on the baking sheet before cooking (add 10 minutes to cooking time) or heat them briefly to crisp them up again if you make them the previous day.

Hollandaise Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 2 egg yolks
  • 3 Tbls lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup butter

Method:

  1. Stir 2 egg yolks, slightly beaten, and 3 tablespoons lemon juice vigorously in 1-quart saucepan with wooden spoon.
  2. Add 1/4 cup butter. Heat over very low heat, stirring constantly, until butter is melted.
  3. Add 1/4 cup butter, stirring vigorously until butter is melted and sauce thickens.
  4. (Be sure butter melts slowly; this give eggs time to cook and thicken sauce without curdling.) Serve hot or at room temperature.

Indian-Style Rice Salad

Time: 30 to 60 minutes, depending on the type of rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 to 2 cups brown or white basmati rice
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallion
  • 3/4 cup cubed cooked potato
  • 3/4 cup cooked cauliflower florets
  • 3/4 cup cooked green peas
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk, or more as needed
  • 3 tablespoons rice wine vinegar, or more to taste
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder, or to taste
  • 1 jalapeno or other hot fresh chili, stemmed, seeded and minced, or to taste, optional
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro.

Method:

  1. Cook rice in abundant salted water, as you would pasta, until it's just done; white rice will take 10 to 15 minutes, brown 30 or a little longer.
  2. Drain, rinse in cold water, drain again, then combine with vegetables in a large bowl.
  3. Combine black pepper, coconut milk, rice wine vinegar and curry powder in a blender and turn machine on; a creamy emulsion will form within 30 seconds. Taste and add more vinegar a teaspoon or two at a time until balance tastes right to you, then add more curry powder, salt, or pepper, if needed.
  4. Drizzle vinaigrette over rice and vegetables.
  5. Use 2 big forks to combine, fluffing rice and tossing gently to separate grains.
  6. Stir in chili, if using, and cilantro, taste, and adjust seasoning or moisten with a little more dressing.
  7. Serve at room temperature, or refrigerate for up to a day, bringing salad back to room temperature before serving.

Yield: 4 to 8 servings.

Persian Carrots

Ingredients

  • salt and pepper; to taste
  • 1 Onion, sliced
  • 4 tb Sultanas or currants
  • 3 lg Or 4 medium carrots
  • 100 ml Water
  • 8 Fresh dates
  • 2 tb olive oil
  • Original recipe makes 4 Servings

Method

  1. This side dish with a difference goes very well with plainly grilled or roasted chicken or braised lamb.
  2. Wash 3 large or 4 medium carrots and slice into rounds.
  3. Peel one onion and slice.
  4. Stone 8 fresh dates (or substitute dried) and cut into slices lengthwise.
  5. Have ready 4 tablespoons of currants or sultanas.
  6. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a wide saucepan. Add all the prepared ingredients and stir-fry for a minute or two, then add 100 mL water, cover and bring to a fast boil. Add salt to taste and put back the lid at an angle so that steam can escape. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are tender and the water has evaporated. If water remains, remove the lid and raise the heat to dry it off.
  7. Serve.

Socca (Farinata)

Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpea flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon, at least, ground black pepper
  • 4 to 6 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 large onion, thinly sliced, optional
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary leaves, optional.

Method

  1. Heat oven to 450 degrees. Put a well-seasoned or nonstick 12-inch pizza pan or cast-iron skillet in oven.
  2. Sift chickpea flour into a bowl.
  3. Add salt and pepper, then slowly add 1 cup lukewarm water, whisking to eliminate lumps.
  4. Stir in 2 tablespoons olive oil.
  5. Cover, and let sit while oven heats, or as long as 12 hours. Batter should be about the consistency of heavy cream.
  6. If using onion and rosemary, stir them into batter.
  7. Pour 2 tablespoons oil into heated pan, and swirl to cover pan evenly.
  8. Pour in batter, and bake 12 to 15 minutes, or until pancake is firm and edges set.
  9. Heat broiler, and brush top of socca with 1 or 2 tablespoons of oil if it looks dry.
  10. Set socca a few inches away from broiler for a few minutes, just long enough to brown it spottily. Cut it into wedges, and serve hot, or at least warm.

Yield: 4 to 6 appetizer servings.

Soup of Bitter Greens with Cheese Dumplings

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup parmesean cheese
  • 1 cup pecorino romano cheese
  • ¾ cup fine dry bread crumbs
  • 1 Tblsp parsley
  • 4 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 Tblsp olive oil
  • ½ pound bitter greens, chopped
  • (e.g., escarole, chickory)
  • 2 quarts broth

Method:

  1. In a large bowl combine parmesean, romano, bread crumbs, parley, eggs and half of the garlic. Stir until a soft dough forms. Using moistened hands, roll the mixture in to ¾ inch balls and arrange in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with plastic wrap. Refrigerate about 30 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, in a medium saucepan, heat the olive oil until shimmering. Add the remaining garlic and cook over moderately high heat, about 30 seconds. Add the greens and cook, stirring, until wilted and just beginning to brown, about 3-4 minutes longer.
  3. Add the stock and bring to simmer. Drop the dumplings into the stock and cook until they rise to the surface, about 5 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and serve in deep bowls, passing extra pecorino at the table.
  5. Make ahead: the uncooked dumplings may be made overnight.

White Corn Tortilla Soup

Ingredients:

  • 3 T Olive Oil
  • 1 1/2 tortillas cut into 1" squares
  • 1 1/2 T minced garlic
  • 2 T minced white onions
  • l 1/2 t minced jalapeno pepper
  • 1 lb white corn kernels
  • 1 1/2 lb chopped tomatoes
  • 1/3 C tomato paste
  • 2 1/2 t ground cumin
  • 1 T Kosher salt
  • 1/8 t ground white pepper
  • 1/2 t chili powder
  • 1 1/2 C water
  • 1 Quart (chicken) stock

Method:

  1. Fry tortilla squares in olive oil until they begin to crisp.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and jalapeno.
  3. Cook 1 - 2 minutes until onion becomes translucent.
  4. Add half the corn and all other ingredients.
  5. Bring soup to a low, even boil.
  6. Boil for 5 minutes; then remove from heat and puree.
  7. Return soup to burner, add reserved corn.
  8. Bring soup to boil again, carefully.
  9. Serve garnished with blue chips, cilantro, or sour cream.
